Biography

I am currently an Associate Research Fellow in the SOCRATES project at Deakin University, Melbourne, focusing on Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) and VPN technologies. I hold a PhD from Deakin University and a Master's degree from Queensland University of Technology, Australia, where my research was funded by the Department of Defense. I have published eight papers, including six in Q1 journals as the first author in top-tier venues such as TDSC, and one in a B-ranked conference. Throughout my academic journey, I have mentored students to publish high-impact papers, contributed to an Elsevier book chapter, and received several prestigious awards, including the School of Information Technology Higher Degree by Research award for the 2022 Academic Year, QUT Dean’s List recognitions in 2019 and 2020, and third place in the 2022 Country to Country CTF (C2C) Cybersecurity Challenge hosted by MIT. I have also secured a successful grant application, won an international prize, and gained extensive experience with industry partners. My teaching roles have been highly rated, with positive feedback from students. My expertise lies in cybersecurity, AI technology, network security, and quantum technology, particularly in machine learning and data science.
